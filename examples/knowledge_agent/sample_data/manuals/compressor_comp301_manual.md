# Maintenance Manual — Air Compressor COMP-301

## Equipment: Atlas Copco GA 55

### 1. General Information

The Atlas Copco GA 55 is an oil-injected rotary screw compressor providing compressed air to the plant's pneumatic systems. It is located in Building B, Utilities area (Asset ID: COMP-301).

- **Free air delivery**: 155 l/s at 7.5 bar
- **Motor power**: 55 kW
- **Operating pressure**: 7.5 bar (max 10 bar)
- **Oil capacity**: 18 litres (Atlas Copco Roto-Inject Fluid)
- **Cooling**: Air-cooled with integrated aftercooler

### 2. Preventive Maintenance Schedule

#### Every 2000 hours
- Replace intake air filter element (FILTER-GA55-INT)
- Check oil level and top up if needed
- Inspect drive belt tension (deflection: 10-15 mm at mid-span)
- Clean condenser/cooler fins with compressed air

#### Every 4000 hours
- Replace oil filter element
- Replace oil separator element
- Change compressor oil (18 litres Atlas Copco Roto-Inject Fluid)
- Check minimum pressure valve operation

#### Every 8000 hours
- Replace intake valve assembly
- Check motor bearings (vibration measurement)
- Inspect coupling and dampers
- Full electrical check: contactors, overloads, sensors

### 3. Air Filter Replacement Procedure

**Required parts**: FILTER-GA55-INT
**Estimated time**: 30 minutes
**Skills required**: Mechanical (basic)

1. Stop the compressor and allow to depressurize (wait 2 minutes).
2. Release the filter housing latch on the intake side.
3. Pull out the old filter element. Inspect for excessive contamination.
4. Insert new filter element. Ensure proper seating in housing.
5. Close the latch. Verify it clicks into place.
6. Start compressor. Verify intake pressure drop is < 25 mbar on display.

**Note**: Replace the filter earlier if the "Filter Service" indicator appears on the Elektronikon controller display.

### 4. Troubleshooting Guide

| Symptom | Probable Cause | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| High intake pressure drop | Clogged filter | Replace intake filter (FILTER-GA55-INT) |
| High oil temperature (> 100°C) | Dirty cooler or low oil | Clean cooler fins. Check oil level. |
| Oil in compressed air | Failed oil separator | Replace separator element |
| Compressor won't start | Electrical fault / high temperature | Check Elektronikon display for fault code |
| Abnormal noise | Bearing wear or intake valve | Schedule inspection |
| Low pressure delivery | Air leak in network | Perform leak audit on distribution piping |

### 5. Safety

⚠️ **Criticality A equipment** — Loss of compressed air stops all pneumatic systems on site.

- Never open pressurized components
- Hot surfaces: oil system can exceed 90°C during operation
- High noise area: wear hearing protection when compressor is running
